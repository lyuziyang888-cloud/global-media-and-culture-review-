# Passport and Institutional Identity

## Case Type

Identity / institutional power / national identity / structure and agency

## What This Case Shows

Woodward uses Madan Sarup's passports to show that official documents assign identities such as nationality, age and occupation while omitting personal feelings, multiple allegiances and historical complexity.

## Analysis Points

- Identity changes over time even when institutional records imply continuity.
- Citizenship is a collective identity backed by state power and legal rights.
- Official categories make some identities visible and omit others.
- A person's identification with a nation may conflict with the identity assigned by the state.
- Institutional identities show how structures constrain agency.

## Exam Sentence

The passport case demonstrates that identity is both personally experienced and institutionally constructed, while official categories cannot fully represent multiple and changing identities.
