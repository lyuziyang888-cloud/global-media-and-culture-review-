# Platform Imperialism

## 30-Second Definition

Platform imperialism is an asymmetrical relationship in which platforms based in dominant countries, especially the United States, control technologies, markets, data and symbolic influence across other countries.

## Keywords To Memorise

- asymmetry
- US dominance
- technological exchange
- capital flows
- global expansion
- symbolic power
- geopolitical power

## Key Features

- A small number of Western platforms own globally important infrastructure.
- Users and advertising revenue flow from non-US markets to US corporations.
- Network effects strengthen existing leaders and weaken local alternatives.
- Corporate expansion is supported by trade policy, lobbying and state power.
- Platform imperialism extends earlier media and cultural imperialism arguments.

## Why It Matters

The concept challenges the idea that digital globalisation disperses power equally. It directs attention to ownership, infrastructure, data, revenue and the state.

## Relationships

- [Platform Capitalism](platform-capitalism.md) provides the profit model.
- [Data Commodification](data-commodification.md) transfers value from global users to platform owners.
- [Platform Power](platform-power.md) becomes geopolitical when it crosses borders.
- [Media Imperialism](media-imperialism.md) is the earlier framework that platform imperialism extends.

## Example

Jin argues that Facebook's global market reach, advertising model, lobbying and relationship with US surveillance power make it a leading platform imperialist.

## Possible Exam Questions

- What is platform imperialism?
- How does platform imperialism extend media imperialism?
- Does digital globalisation distribute power equally?
