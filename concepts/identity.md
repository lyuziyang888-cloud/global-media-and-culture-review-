# Identity

## 30-Second Definition

Identity is the socially shaped understanding of who an individual or group is, formed through self-identification, recognition by others, similarity, difference and representation.

## Keywords To Memorise

- personal and social
- identification
- similarity and difference
- multiple identities
- fluidity
- structure and agency
- recognition

## Key Features

- Identity links how people see themselves with how others see them.
- People actively take up identities, but their choices are socially constrained.
- Identity is marked by belonging to some groups and difference from others.
- Individuals can hold several identities that may conflict.
- Identities can change rather than remain fixed.

## Why It Matters

Identity connects individual experience to institutions, culture and power. It helps explain how people locate themselves in the social world and how categories can enable, restrict or exclude them.

## Example

Woodward's passport example shows that official documents assign nationality and legal status but cannot capture a person's multiple identities, feelings or changing self-understanding.
