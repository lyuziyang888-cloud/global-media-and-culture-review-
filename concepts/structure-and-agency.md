# Structure and Agency

## 30-Second Definition

Structure and agency describe the tension between social forces that shape or restrict identity and the capacity of individuals or groups to act, interpret roles and reconstruct identities.

## Keywords To Memorise

- constraint
- choice
- institutions
- social roles
- resources
- resistance
- collective action

## Key Features

- Structures include institutions, cultural expectations, economic conditions and established roles.
- Agency is real but never unlimited.
- Individuals can interpret and negotiate social roles.
- Collective action can challenge stereotypes and change structures.

## Why It Matters

The framework avoids treating identity as either completely chosen or completely imposed. It shows that identity emerges through an ongoing interaction between constraint and action.

## Relationships

- [Identity](identity.md) is produced through both structure and agency.
- [Representation](representation.md) can operate as a constraint or a resource for resistance.
- Institutions can make some identities possible and others difficult or impossible.

## Example

Woodward's body-project examples show people using their bodies to challenge gender and age stereotypes, while still acting within biological and cultural limits.

## Possible Exam Questions

- What is the difference between structure and agency?
- How do structure and agency interact in identity formation?
- Can people freely choose their identities?
