# Media Regulation

## 30-Second Definition

Media regulation refers to government rules, policies and institutions that shape how media industries operate, fund content and serve public or cultural goals.

## Keywords To Memorise

- policy
- government
- funding
- local content
- public interest
- cultural protection
- platform obligations

## Key Features

- Can support domestic media industries.
- Can require or encourage local content.
- Can fund underrepresented communities.
- Can create conflict with large global platforms.

## Why It Matters

Media regulation matters because global media markets do not automatically protect local culture, national identity or minority representation.

## Relationships

- Media regulation supports [Local Content](local-content.md).
- Media regulation responds to [Platform Power](platform-power.md).
- Media regulation can protect [Cultural Identity](cultural-identity.md).
- Media regulation shapes media industry economics.
