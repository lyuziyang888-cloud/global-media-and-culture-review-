# Platform Capitalism

## 30-Second Definition

Platform capitalism is a business system in which privately owned digital platforms mediate interactions, collect user data and convert attention, activity and data into profit.

## Keywords To Memorise

- private infrastructure
- mediation
- users
- data extraction
- advertising
- commodification
- profit

## Key Features

- Platforms connect users, advertisers, developers and service providers.
- They actively shape interactions rather than acting as neutral intermediaries.
- Users create content, activity and data that platforms monetise.
- Revenue depends heavily on targeted advertising and expanding user networks.

## Why It Matters

Platform capitalism reveals the vertical commercial power hidden beneath apparently horizontal social interaction.

## Relationships

- Platform capitalism relies on [Data Commodification](data-commodification.md).
- It is a foundation of [Platform Imperialism](platform-imperialism.md).
- It expands [Platform Power](platform-power.md) through ownership, data and network effects.

## Example

Facebook provides social interaction while aggregating user attention and data to sell targeted access to advertisers.
