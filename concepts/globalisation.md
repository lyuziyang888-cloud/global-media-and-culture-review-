# Globalisation

## 30-Second Definition

Globalisation refers to the increasing interconnectedness of people, economies, cultures, technologies and media across national borders.

## Keywords To Memorise

- interconnectedness
- cross-border flows
- media circulation
- cultural exchange
- technology
- global market
- local culture

## Key Features

- Media content can travel across national borders quickly.
- Global companies can distribute content to many countries.
- Local cultures may become more visible internationally.
- Local cultures may also be shaped by dominant global industries.

## Why It Matters

Globalisation is important because it explains how media industries, audiences and cultures are connected beyond one nation. It helps explain why a Canadian production, a Korean-themed film, Netflix, HBO and local cultural policy can all be part of the same media question.

- What is globalisation?
- Explain the relationship between globalisation and local culture.
- Discuss how global streaming platforms affect national media industries.
- Does globalisation support or threaten cultural identity?

## Common Mistake

Do not define globalisation only as "countries becoming connected." Add media, culture, cross-border flows and power.
