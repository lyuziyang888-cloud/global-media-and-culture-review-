# Global Media

## 30-Second Definition

Global media refers to media content, industries, platforms and audiences that operate across national and cultural boundaries.

## Keywords To Memorise

- cross-border media
- global audiences
- distribution
- platforms
- culture
- industry
- power

## Key Features

- Produced, distributed or consumed across multiple countries.
- Often shaped by global media companies and streaming platforms.
- Can still contain local, national or regional identity.
- Raises questions about ownership, representation and cultural influence.

## Why It Matters

Global media is the central concept of the course. It helps explain why media cannot be understood only through one nation, one culture or one company.

## Relationships

- Global media is shaped by [Globalisation](globalisation.md).
- Global media can carry [Local Content](local-content.md).
- Global media influences [Representation](representation.md).
- Global media creates questions about [Western Media](western-media.md).

## Example

A Canadian series distributed through HBO or Netflix can be local in production and global in circulation.

## Possible Exam Questions

- What is global media?
- How can media be both local and global?
- Why is it difficult to define the nationality of a media product?
