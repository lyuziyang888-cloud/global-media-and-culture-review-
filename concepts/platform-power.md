# Platform Power

## 30-Second Definition

Platform power refers to the ability of digital platforms to influence which content is funded, distributed, promoted and accessed by audiences.

## Keywords To Memorise

- distribution power
- visibility
- funding
- audience access
- algorithms
- market power
- regulation

## Key Features

- Platforms can decide what becomes visible to audiences.
- Platforms can expand local content internationally.
- Platforms can create dependence on foreign companies.
- Platform power often leads to government regulation debates.

## Why It Matters

Platform power matters because global media is not only about content travelling. It is also about who controls the routes through which content travels.

## Relationships

- Platform power shapes [Streaming Platforms](streaming-platforms.md).
- Its economic model can be explained through [Platform Capitalism](platform-capitalism.md).
- Its unequal global expansion can become [Platform Imperialism](platform-imperialism.md).
- It depends on the collection and [Commodification of Data](data-commodification.md).
- Platform power affects [Local Content](local-content.md).
- Platform power can influence [Representation](representation.md).
- Platform power is challenged by [Media Regulation](media-regulation.md).

## Example

Netflix and HBO can give Canadian productions global visibility, but their influence also raises questions about whether foreign platforms should financially support domestic media industries.

Facebook shows a broader form of platform power: control over user data, advertising markets, communication infrastructure and political access.
