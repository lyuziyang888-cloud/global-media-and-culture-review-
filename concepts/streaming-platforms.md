# Streaming Platforms

## 30-Second Definition

Streaming platforms are digital services that distribute media content online to audiences, often across national borders.

## Keywords To Memorise

- Netflix
- HBO
- Crave
- distribution
- audience access
- global reach
- platform power

## Key Features

- They help local productions reach wider audiences.
- They can influence what content is funded, promoted and watched.
- They often operate across national borders.
- They create regulatory challenges for governments.

## Why It Matters

Streaming platforms matter because they connect local media industries to global audiences while also concentrating distribution power in large companies.

## Relationships

- Streaming platforms are part of [Global Media](global-media.md).
- Streaming platforms increase [Platform Power](platform-power.md).
- Streaming platforms affect [Local Content](local-content.md).
- Streaming platforms are targeted by [Media Regulation](media-regulation.md).

## Example

Netflix can help a Canadian or Indigenous-led production circulate globally, but Canadian regulators may also ask platforms to support local production financially.

## Possible Exam Questions

- How do streaming platforms affect local media industries?
- Do global platforms support or threaten local culture?
- Why do governments regulate streaming platforms?
