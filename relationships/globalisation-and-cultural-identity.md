# Globalisation and Cultural Identity

## Relationship In One Sentence

Globalisation can spread local cultural identity to global audiences, but it can also weaken or reshape identity through dominant global media industries.

## A: Globalisation

Globalisation creates cross-border flows of media, culture, technology and capital.

## B: Cultural Identity

Cultural identity gives groups shared meanings, symbols, stories and a sense of belonging.

## How They Interact

```text
Globalisation
↓
Global platforms and media flows
↓
Local content reaches wider audiences
↓
Cultural identity becomes more visible
```

But:

```text
Globalisation
↓
Dominant media industries
↓
Local culture may become less distinct
↓
Cultural identity may be reshaped
```

## Case Study

Canada's screen industry wants global reach while maintaining distinct Canadian cultural identity.

## Exam Answer Skeleton

Globalisation increases the movement of media across borders. This can support cultural identity by allowing local stories to reach wider audiences. However, it can also create pressure for local media to fit dominant global or American media styles. The Canada screen industry case shows this tension because Canadian productions seek international distribution while still trying to remain culturally Canadian.
