# Structure and Agency in Identity

## Relationship In One Sentence

Identities are neither freely chosen nor completely imposed: agency operates within social, institutional, cultural, economic and bodily constraints.

## How They Interact

```text
Social structures provide roles and limits
<->
Individuals interpret, perform and resist
<->
Collective action can reshape structures
```

## Case Study

Woodward's passport example shows institutional classification, while body projects and social movements show people challenging imposed meanings and stereotypes.

## Evaluation

Agency matters, but available resources and recognition are unequal. A convincing answer should avoid claiming either total freedom or total determination.

## Possible Exam Questions

- Explain the relationship between structure, agency and identity.
- To what extent can individuals choose their identities?
- How can collective action reconstruct identity?

## Source Anchor

Woodward, "Questions of Identity," pp. 6-11, 18-23 and 34-39.
