# Representation and Identity

## Relationship In One Sentence

Representation shapes identity because media portrayals influence how people, communities and nations understand themselves and are understood by others.

## A: Representation

Representation is how people, places and cultures are portrayed in media.

## B: Identity

Identity is how individuals or groups understand who they are and where they belong.

## How They Interact

```text
Media representation
↓
Visibility and meaning
↓
Audience understanding
↓
Cultural or national identity
```

The relationship also works in the other direction: people use available symbols, stories and performances to communicate identity, while their social position affects how those representations are interpreted.

## Case Study

`North of North` is useful for Indigenous representation because it foregrounds Indigenous creators, performers, locations and stories.

Woodward's passport example shows how an institution represents identity through a fixed legal category even when a person's attachments and self-understanding are multiple.

## Exam Answer Skeleton

Representation and identity are closely connected. Representations provide the symbols and categories through which identities become recognisable. People can use these resources to express themselves, but institutions and media producers have unequal power to define the available categories. Representation can therefore affirm identity or simplify, exclude and stereotype it.
