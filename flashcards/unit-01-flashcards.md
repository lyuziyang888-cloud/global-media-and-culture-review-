# Unit 1 Flashcards

## Globalisation

Q: What is globalisation?

A: The increasing interconnectedness of people, economies, cultures, technologies and media across national borders.

## Global Media

Q: What is global media?

A: Media content, industries, platforms and audiences that operate across national and cultural boundaries.

## Local Content

Q: What is local content?

A: Media connected to a particular place, culture, community or nation through production, stories, creators, locations, language or themes.

## Cultural Identity

Q: What is cultural identity?

A: Shared meanings, values, symbols, stories and practices through which a group understands itself.

## Representation

Q: What is representation?

A: How people, places, cultures, identities and social groups are portrayed in media.

## Platform Power

Q: What is platform power?

A: The ability of digital platforms to influence which content is funded, distributed, promoted and accessed by audiences.

## Media Regulation

Q: Why does media regulation matter?

A: It can support local content, protect cultural identity and respond to the power of global platforms.

## Western Media

Q: Why is Western media difficult to define?

A: Because "the West" is a contested category shaped by geography, culture, history and power.

## Canada Case

Q: What does the Canada screen industry case show?

A: The tension between global media reach and the protection of local or national cultural identity.

## Heated Rivalry

Q: Why is `Heated Rivalry` useful?

A: It helps analyse what makes media Canadian, including cast, crew, creator, location, funding, story and audience.

## North of North

Q: Why is `North of North` useful?

A: It links Indigenous-led representation with global distribution through streaming platforms.
