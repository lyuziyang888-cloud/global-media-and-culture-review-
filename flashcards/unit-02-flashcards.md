# Unit 2 Flashcards

Use these for active recall. Cover the answer before responding.

## Identity

**Q: What is identity?**  
A: A person's or group's sense of who they are, formed through self-identification, recognition by others and social categories.

**Q: Why is identity different from personality?**  
A: Identity connects personal experience to social positions, group membership, institutions and active identification.

**Q: Why is identity relational?**  
A: Identity is produced through similarity with some people and difference from others.

**Q: What can make difference unequal?**  
A: Power can make one identity appear normal or dominant while marking another as inferior, deviant or outside.

**Q: What is structure?**  
A: The institutions, categories, roles and unequal resources that shape available identity positions.

**Q: What is agency?**  
A: The capacity to interpret, negotiate, perform, resist or transform identity positions.

**Q: What does the passport case demonstrate?**  
A: An institution can fix identity into a legal national category that does not capture a person's multiple attachments or self-understanding.

## Platform Power

**Q: What is platform capitalism?**  
A: A business model in which platforms mediate interactions and turn user activity, attention and data into economic value.

**Q: What is data commodification?**  
A: Converting personal information and behaviour into assets that can be analysed, traded or used for profit.

**Q: What are network effects?**  
A: A platform becomes more valuable as more users join, helping dominant platforms become harder to challenge.

**Q: What is platform imperialism?**  
A: The unequal global expansion of platform corporations that concentrates economic, political and cultural power in dominant countries, especially the United States in Jin's account.

**Q: How does platform imperialism differ from platform capitalism?**  
A: Capitalism describes the accumulation model; imperialism describes its unequal cross-border power relations.

**Q: Why are platforms not neutral intermediaries?**  
A: They set rules, collect data, rank visibility, control access and pursue commercial and political interests.

**Q: Does platform power eliminate the nation-state?**  
A: No. Jin argues that corporate and state power can reinforce each other through policy, lobbying, surveillance and geopolitical interests.

**Q: What is the key caution when using Jin's statistics?**  
A: They describe the period before the chapter's 2019 publication and should not be presented as current figures.
