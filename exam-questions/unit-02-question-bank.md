# Unit 2 Question Bank

## Definition and Recall

1. What is identity?
2. What is difference, and why is it relational?
3. Define structure and agency.
4. What is platform capitalism?
5. What is data commodification?
6. What is platform imperialism?

## Explain Relationships

1. Explain the relationship between identity and difference.
2. How do representation and institutions shape identity?
3. Explain the relationship between structure and agency in identity formation.
4. How does data commodification support platform capitalism?
5. Explain the relationship between platform imperialism and the nation-state.

## Compare

1. Compare personal identity and social identity.
2. Compare self-identification with identity assigned by others.
3. Compare platform power, platform capitalism and platform imperialism.
4. Compare media imperialism with platform imperialism.

## Discuss and Evaluate

1. "Individuals are free to choose their identities." Discuss.
2. To what extent does difference create belonging and exclusion?
3. Evaluate the claim that digital platforms are neutral intermediaries.
4. Does the global rise of Facebook make nation-states less important? Discuss.
5. To what extent does Facebook reproduce imperial relationships?
6. How might platform power affect the construction and representation of identity?

## Evidence Prompts

For every answer, add:

```text
direct answer
-> precise concept definition
-> mechanism or relationship
-> evidence from Woodward or Jin
-> limitation or counterargument
-> judgement
```

Useful limitations include the multiplicity of identities, the capacity for agency and resistance, the continued power of states, the growth of non-US platforms, and the historical date of Jin's market statistics.
